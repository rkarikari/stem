Section 01

The Market Opportunity

The BECE examination is a compulsory national exit from Junior High School in Ghana. It is sat by every JHS 3 student in the country, making the BECE prep market one of the largest and most predictable education markets in West Africa. Crucially, the market grows reliably every year.

The 620,000 JHS 3 candidates represent the immediate addressable market for revision resources. The broader market includes ~1.24 million JHS 1 and 2 students who will face the same exam in coming years — and parents and teachers who buy resources on students' behalf. This catalogue covers all nine BECE examination subjects: English Language, Mathematics, Integrated Science, Social Studies, Religious & Moral Education (RME), French, Career Technology, Computing, and Creative Arts — giving every student a single source for complete BECE preparation.

📊

If just 1% of 2026 BECE candidates purchase each title at GHS 30, that is 6,200 copies per book, generating GHS 186,000 (~USD 12,400) in gross revenue per title — GHS 1.67 million across all 9 titles. At 2%, revenue doubles. Families purchasing the full catalogue (all 9 subjects) at the bundle price represent an average order value of GHS 200+, the highest in the Ghanaian BECE prep market.

Optimal Pricing Strategy

Pricing must balance two competing forces: affordability for Ghanaian JHS families (many earning below GHS 2,000/month) and perceived value as a serious exam tool. Underpricing signals low quality; overpricing cuts off the mass market. Research on comparable Ghanaian educational resources and BECE prep materials points to a clear sweet spot.

Bundle promotions (Core 5 at GHS 100 or Full 9 at GHS 180) during exam season can significantly lift average order value and total revenue. This mirrors how physical revision books are discounted at shops near exam season — but with the additional advantage that bundles cost nothing extra to fulfil.

Mobile-first is non-negotiable. In 2024, Ghana processed 8.1 billion mobile money transactions — a figure that jumped 18.9% year-on-year. Section 06

Marketing Strategy

The BECE prep market is driven by three buyer personas: (A) the student (self-directed, social media native), (B) the parent (decision-maker, WhatsApp and Facebook user), and (C) the teacher (influencer and recommender — the single highest-leverage channel). Marketing must speak to all three.

01

Teacher Seeding (Highest ROI)

Identify 50 influential JHS teachers across all 16 regions via GES school directories and Facebook teacher groups — targeting at least 5 per subject area. Send the relevant free title with a personalised note. Offer a 15% referral commission on every copy their students purchase via a unique Selar affiliate link.

  • One teacher with 35 students can generate 10–20 sales
  • Cost: 50 free copies (GHS 0) + ~GHS 200 commission
  • Potential return: GHS 10,000–30,000 per cohort
02

WhatsApp Broadcast Network

Build and maintain 3–5 WhatsApp broadcast lists: (1) JHS teachers, (2) JHS 3 students, (3) Parents of JHS students, (4) SHS students (for WASSCE expansion). Share exam tips, free mini-quizzes, and BECE reminders weekly — then convert with a purchase link.

  • Build lists via Instagram bio links and Facebook posts
  • Send free "Grade A Tip of the Week" to build trust first
  • Link each tip back to the full eBook at checkout
03

Facebook & Instagram Ads (Targeted)

Run Facebook Ads targeting: Ghana + Nigeria, age 13–18 + parents of school-age children, interest in WAEC/BECE/GES/education. Budget GHS 50/day for 2 weeks before peak revision season (March–May). Use student testimonials and Grade C vs Grade A comparison screenshots as creatives — these are highly shareable.

  • Expected CPM: GHS 3–8 (Ghanaian education audience)
  • Target cost per sale: GHS 8–15 (leaves GHS 12–22 net margin)
04

TikTok & YouTube Shorts

Create 30–60 second short videos: "The WAEC trick examiners never tell you," "Grade A vs Grade C answer — spot the difference," "1 minute Social Studies revision." These formats drive massive organic reach among JHS students. Link in bio to Selar store. Each viral video can generate hundreds of organic sales.

  • Cost: GHS 0 (organic) to GHS 300 (boosted)
  • Content ideas: exam mistakes, mnemonic reveals, mark schemes
05

School Bulk Licensing

Approach private JHS schools directly for institutional licences. Offer: GHS 1,500 for the complete 9-subject library for all JHS 3 students at a school for one academic year, or GHS 800 for the Core 5 compulsory subjects only. 100 schools = GHS 150,000 — achievable via one targeted school email campaign to GES-registered private schools.

  • Start with private schools (faster decision-making)
  • Provide a school "resource pack" with usage guide for teachers
  • Offer free trial for one class to demonstrate value
06

West African WAEC Expansion

The same WAEC curriculum structure applies in Nigeria (with minor variations), Sierra Leone, Liberia and The Gambia. Combined, these nations produce over 1.5 million WAEC candidates annually. A lightly adapted edition (or the existing one with a note on syllabus alignment) opens a market 3× the size of Ghana's.

  • Nigeria: list on Selar (dominant in Nigeria too) and Flutterwave store
  • Price in USD ($3.99) for international West African buyers

Piracy / Unauthorized sharing HIGH HIGH Keep price low (GHS 30 is below piracy motivation threshold). Add personalization (buyer's name embedded). Focus on volume over DRM. Offer annual editions with new content so old copies become outdated.
WAEC curriculum change MEDIUM HIGH The 2025–26 curriculum transition is already reflected in the books' content. Monitor GES syllabus updates annually. Low-cost annual epub revisions keep content current.
Low digital literacy / no ebook reader MEDIUM MEDIUM Epub files open in free apps (Moon+ Reader, Google Play Books) on any Android phone. Offer a PDF version as a free alternative format. Most JHS students have smartphones or access to family devices.

Free resources offer past questions only — not exam strategy and understanding. Position accordingly.
Slow initial sales / cash flow MEDIUM LOW Break-even requires only 26 sales. Startup cost is under GHS 700. Risk exposure is minimal. Focus first 30 days on organic (free) channels — WhatsApp, teacher seeding — before spending on ads.

This nine-title catalogue is positioned to be the most complete and intelligent BECE prep resource suite on the Ghanaian market. Covering every examinable subject — from Mathematics and Integrated Science to Creative Arts and French — it addresses every student's need in a single publisher. The combination of an enormous, growing, exam-driven captive market; near-zero marginal cost per copy; powerful bundle economics (GHS 200 for the full set vs GHS 270 separately); and a GHS 700 maximum startup investment makes this one of the most capital-efficient education publishing opportunities in West Africa. The primary success factor is not production cost — it is distribution speed, teacher adoption, and bundle marketing. Launching within the academic term, seeding subject teachers first, and offering the Full 9-Subject Bundle as an anchor product are the three decisions that will determine Year 1 outcomes.
