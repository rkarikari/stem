Building a 2m Dipole

Materials Needed:

  • 4mm copper wire (2√ó 50cm lengths)
  • SO-239 coax connector
  • PVC or plastic center insulator
  • 50Œ© coax cable (RG-58 or RG-8X)

Construction Steps:

  1. Calculate length: 143/146 = 0.979m total ‚Üí 49cm each side
  2. Cut wire: Add 5cm extra for tuning
  3. Solder: Attach one wire to center pin, other to shield
  4. Mount: Install center insulator, orient horizontally
  5. Test SWR: Check 144-148 MHz range
  6. Tune: Trim small amounts to minimize SWR

Expected Performance:

  • SWR < 1.5:1 across 2m band
  • Omni-directional in horizontal plane
  • Ideal height: Œª/2 above ground (1m minimum)

Matching Techniques

  • Balun: 1:1 (choke), 4:1, 9:1 transformers
  • Gamma Match: Variable tap on driven element
  • Beta Match: Folded dipole configuration
  • Antenna Tuner: LC network at shack end

Antenna Testing

SWR Measurement Procedure

  1. Connect SWR meter between radio and antenna
  2. Set radio to 1W output
  3. Sweep frequency across band in 100 kHz steps
  4. Record SWR at each frequency
  5. Plot SWR curve
  6. Identify resonant frequency (minimum SWR)
  7. Adjust antenna length if resonance is off-center

Physical Interpretation

The dramatic performance differences are rooted in fundamental antenna physics:

üî¨ Why SRH-770S Excels on 2m

At approximately 70cm physical length, the SRH-770S approaches an optimal electrical length on 2m (~3/8 wavelength), resulting in favorable radiation resistance and high efficiency.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why SRH-77CA Fails on 2m

The compact SRH-77CA is electrically very short on VHF, leading to extremely low radiation resistance and high loss. Most transmitter power is dissipated as heat rather than radiated.

‚úÖ Why Both Work on 70cm

At UHF frequencies, even compact antennas achieve reasonable electrical lengths. Both antennas fall within 3.6-4.6 dB of the directional Elk reference.

Final Conclusions

üìä Key Findings Summary

  1. Band-specific optimization matters: No single handheld antenna excels on both bands
  2. VHF requires physical length: Compact antennas suffer catastrophic losses on 2m
  3. Power is secondary to antenna: 5√ó power increase cannot overcome poor antenna design
  4. Measurements trump marketing: Manufacturer specifications don't reflect real-world performance
