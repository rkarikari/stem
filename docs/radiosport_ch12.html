Phase 1: Site Survey

RF Environment:

  • 360¬∞ horizon clearance assessment
  • Noise floor measurement on all bands
  • Line-of-sight verification for VHF/UHF
  • Identify power line interference sources

Logistics:

  • Vehicle access for equipment transport
  • Level ground for setup (10m √ó 10m minimum)
  • Anchor points for guy wires
  • Drainage patterns (avoid flooding)

Solar Resources:

  • Distance to hospital (<30 min preferred)

Field Setup Procedures

Solar Power System Setup

‚òÄÔ∏è 45-Minute Solar Deployment

Step 1: Panel Positioning (10 min)

  1. Identify optimal location (southern exposure)
  2. Set angle to latitude ¬± 15¬∞ (seasonal adjustment)
  3. Orient true south using compass
  4. Secure panels (stakes, weights, guy wires)

Step 2: Battery Setup (10 min)

  1. Place in shaded, ventilated enclosure
  2. Verify voltage with multimeter (12.8-13.2V)
  3. Connect monitoring display

Step 3: Controller Installation (10 min)

  1. Mount in shaded location
  2. Connect battery FIRST (red +, black -)
  3. Install 30A fuse on positive lead
  4. Configure for LiFePO4 chemistry

Step 4: Panel Connection (10 min)

  1. Connect panels to controller (MC4 connectors)
  2. Verify charging current appears
  3. Check green charging LED

Step 5: Load Distribution (5 min)

  1. Connect Powerpole distribution panel
  2. Verify 12.8V output
  3. Test with small load before full ops

VHF/UHF Station Setup

üìª Radio Station Assembly (60 minutes)

Antenna Installation (30 min):

  1. Erect 6m telescoping mast with tripod base
  2. Attach dual-band vertical to mast top
  3. Install 3 guy wires at 120¬∞ spacing
  4. Route coax with drip loop at base
  5. Test SWR on 2m and 70cm bands

Radio Configuration (20 min):

  1. Mount transceiver securely on table
  2. Connect 12V power with inline fuse
  3. Attach coax and ground system
  4. Program repeater frequencies
  5. Test TX/RX on simplex frequency

Final Checks (10 min):

Operational Best Practices

Team Roles

Assign Clear Responsibilities:

  • Operations Manager: Coordinates activities, maintains schedule
  • Radio Operators (2-3): Rotate shifts, maintain logs
  • Safety Officer: Monitors weather, equipment, personnel
  • Drone Pilot: Flight operations, battery management
  • Power Manager: Solar system, battery monitoring
  • Logger: Contact documentation, data recording

Emergency Procedures

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Situations:

Weather Emergency (Lightning):

  • Cease all operations immediately
  • Lower antennas if time permits
  • Take shelter in vehicle or building
  • Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before resuming

Equipment Failure:

  • Switch to backup systems
  • Document failure for post-op analysis
  • Do not attempt field repairs on complex equipment

Medical Emergency:

  • Designate first aid responder
  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Provide GPS coordinates to dispatchers
  • Keep patient warm and comfortable
