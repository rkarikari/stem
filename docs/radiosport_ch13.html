CHAPTER 13

⚠️ Safety Protocols & Regulations

Safety is paramount in RadioSport. This chapter covers RF exposure, electrical hazards, antenna safety, drone operations, field safety, and regulatory compliance. Zero tolerance policy for unsafe behavior.

RF Safety and Exposure Limits

⚠️ RF Radiation Hazards

Radio frequency energy can cause tissue heating and other biological effects at high power densities. Amateur radio operators must ensure safe RF exposure levels.

FCC/ICNIRP Exposure Limits (Controlled Environment):

Frequency Range Power Density (mW/cm²) Electric Field (V/m)
HF (3-30 MHz) 1.0 614 / f(MHz)
VHF (30-300 MHz) 1.0 61.4
UHF (300-3000 MHz) f(MHz) / 300 61.4

Safe Distance Calculations

Minimum Safe Distance Formula:

Near-Field (< λ/2π from antenna):

Use worst-case assumption or detailed modeling

Far-Field (> λ/2π from antenna):

Power Density = (P × G) / (4π × d²)

Example: 50W VHF at 146 MHz

  • Power: 50W
  • Antenna gain: 3 dBd (2× power gain)
  • Effective radiated power: 100W
  • Safe distance (1 mW/cm²): d = √(100 / 4π × 0.001) = 2.8 meters

Rule of Thumb: Stay at least 3 meters from transmitting VHF/UHF antenna at 50W

RF Safety Practices

  • ✅ Never stand in front of directional antenna while transmitting
  • ✅ Keep transmitting antennas away from living spaces
  • ✅ Use minimum power necessary for communications
  • ✅ Install RF warning signs at base of antenna masts
  • ✅ Educate family/neighbors about safe distances
  • ✅ Limit continuous high-power transmissions (duty cycle)
  • ✅ Ground all equipment properly to prevent RF burns

Electrical Safety

🔌 Critical Electrical Hazards

DC Power Hazards:

  • Short Circuit Risk: 12V battery can deliver 500+ amps instantaneously
  • Arc Flash: DC arcs are sustained (unlike AC), can melt copper wire
  • Fire Risk: Undersized wiring causes resistive heating and fire
  • Jewelry Hazard: Metal rings/bracelets can complete circuit across terminals

Mandatory Safety Rules:

  1. Fusing: Install appropriate fuse within 18" of battery positive terminal
  2. No Jewelry: Remove ALL rings, watches, bracelets, necklaces before working with power
  3. Insulated Tools: Use insulated screwdrivers and wrenches
  4. One Hand Rule: Work with one hand when possible to prevent current path across heart
  5. Polarity Check: ALWAYS verify positive/negative before connecting (use multimeter)
  6. Disconnect First: Disconnect negative terminal first when removing battery

Battery Safety

LiFePO4 Safety:

  • ✅ Use batteries with built-in BMS (Battery Management System)
  • ✅ Never charge below 0°C (32°F) - causes lithium plating
  • ✅ Store at 50-60% SOC for long-term
  • ✅ Monitor cell voltages (should be within 0.05V of each other)
  • ⛔ Do not puncture, crush, or disassemble

LiPo Battery Safety (Drones):

  • ✅ Store in LiPo safety bag or metal ammo can
  • ✅ Never charge unattended
  • ✅ Check for puffing/swelling (sign of failure)
  • ✅ Storage charge: 3.8V per cell
  • ⛔ NEVER discharge below 3.0V per cell
  • ⛔ If fire occurs: DO NOT USE WATER - use sand, dry powder extinguisher, or LiPo bag

Antenna Installation Safety

⚡ Tower and Antenna Hazards

Falls from Height:

  • Risk: Serious injury or death from falling
  • Prevention: Use proper fall arrest equipment when climbing >2m
  • Rule: Never climb alone; always have spotter

Falling Objects:

  • Risk: Tools, hardware, antennas falling on people below
  • Prevention: Establish work zone, hard hats required, tool lanyards

Electrical Contact:

  • Risk: Antenna touching power lines = FATAL
  • Prevention: Maintain 10-foot clearance from ALL power lines
  • Rule: If antenna falls toward power line, ABANDON IT and call utility

Safe Antenna Raising Procedure

✓ Step-by-Step Safety Protocol:

  1. Pre-Raise Inspection:
    • Check all bolts and connections tight
    • Inspect mast for cracks or damage
    • Verify guy wire anchors secure
    • Confirm no overhead power lines within fall radius
  2. Team Briefing:
    • Assign roles: 3+ people for mast >3m
    • Establish clear hand signals (verbal hard to hear)
    • Identify "abort" signal (stop immediately)
    • Clear work area of all non-essential personnel
  3. Raising:
    • Lift slowly and steadily
    • Person on guy wires maintains tension
    • Stop if anything unexpected happens
    • Once vertical, secure immediately
  4. Final Checks:
    • Mast plumb (use level or plumb bob)
    • Guy wires equal tension
    • No wobble when lightly pushed
    • Coax properly secured to mast

Drone Safety Protocols

🚁 UAS Operating Rules

Pre-Flight Safety Checklist:

  • ✅ Airspace check (no restricted zones, airports)
  • ✅ Weather assessment (wind < 10 m/s, no rain, good visibility)
  • ✅ Battery voltage check (>3.7V per cell for LiPo)
  • ✅ Props inspection (no cracks, secure attachment)
  • ✅ GPS lock confirmed (8+ satellites)
  • ✅ Return-to-home altitude set
  • ✅ Spotter assigned (watch for aircraft)
  • ✅ First aid kit accessible

In-Flight Rules:

  • ⛔ Maximum altitude: 120m (400 feet) AGL
  • ⛔ Visual line of sight (VLOS) mandatory
  • ⛔ Do not fly over people
  • ⛔ Yield right-of-way to manned aircraft immediately
  • ⛔ Reserve 30% battery for return-to-home
  • ✅ Maintain control at all times
  • ✅ Land immediately if control issues

Emergency Procedures:

  • Lost Signal: Drone should RTH automatically (verify beforehand)
  • Low Battery: Land immediately in safe area, don't push limits
  • Flyaway: If unresponsive, activate RTH or emergency motor stop as last resort
  • Crash: Secure scene, check for injuries, disconnect battery, document for analysis

Field Operation Safety

Weather Hazards

Lightning Safety:

  • Rule: If you can hear thunder, lightning is close enough to strike
  • Action: Cease operations, lower antennas, take shelter in vehicle or building
  • 30-30 Rule: Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before resuming
  • Never: Seek shelter under isolated trees (lightning attractors)

Heat Safety:

  • Drink water regularly (before feeling thirsty)
  • Shade canopy for operators
  • Take breaks every 2 hours
  • Watch for heat exhaustion signs (dizziness, nausea, confusion)

First Aid Essentials

Injury Type Common Causes First Aid Response
Minor Cuts Wire ends, sharp metal Clean with water, apply bandage, monitor for infection
Burns Soldering iron, hot resistors Cool water 10+ minutes, sterile dressing, seek medical if severe
Electrical Shock Improper wiring, wet conditions Disconnect power, check breathing/pulse, call emergency services, CPR if needed
Eye Injury Flying debris, chemical splash Flush with clean water 15+ min, cover eye, seek immediate medical attention
Sprain/Strain Lifting heavy equipment, falls RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation. Immobilize if severe.

Regulatory Compliance

Amateur Radio Licensing

License Requirements:

Students must obtain appropriate amateur radio license before transmitting.

License Class Exam Privileges
Technician 35 questions Full VHF/UHF, limited HF
General 35 questions Most HF bands, all VHF/UHF
Amateur Extra 50 questions All amateur privileges

Note: Under supervision, unlicensed students can operate under control of licensed RadioSport instructor.

Operating Regulations

  • ✅ Identify station every 10 minutes and at end of contact
  • ✅ Use only authorized frequencies for license class
  • ✅ Respect power limits (typically 1500W PEP for HF, 50W typical for VHF/UHF)
  • ✅ No music, broadcasting, or encrypted messages
  • ✅ Emergency communications have priority
  • ⛔ No profanity or obscenity
  • ⛔ No business or commercial use
  • ⛔ No third-party traffic to prohibited countries

Chapter Summary

  • ✅ RF safety: maintain safe distances, use minimum power
  • ✅ Electrical: proper fusing, wire sizing, no jewelry
  • ✅ Batteries: appropriate chemistry for application, safe storage
  • ✅ Antennas: team lifting, power line clearance, fall protection
  • ✅ Drones: VLOS, altitude limits, battery reserves
  • ✅ Field ops: weather awareness, first aid, emergency procedures
  • ✅ Regulations: proper licensing, frequency privileges, station ID

⚠️ Safety Culture

Zero tolerance for unsafe behavior. Safety is everyone's responsibility. When in doubt, STOP and ask for guidance.
