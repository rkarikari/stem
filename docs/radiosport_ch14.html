Performance-Based Award System

Three-Tier Award Structure

Tier 1: Weekly Micro Awards (Value: $2-10)

Criteria: Specific accomplishments during weekly sessions

  • First successful VHF contact: RF connector set (BNC, PL-259)
  • Complete Python script: USB cable or component kit
  • Perfect attendance (month): 10m coax cable
  • Help another student: Soldering supplies
  • Safety excellence: Safety glasses or multimeter

Purpose: Immediate reinforcement, build component collection

Tier 2: Monthly Achievement Awards (Value: $30-80)

Criteria: Major milestones and project completion

  • License earned (Technician): Handheld VHF/UHF transceiver
  • Antenna project completion: SWR meter or antenna analyzer
  • Solar system operational: Charge controller or battery
  • Satellite contact made: RTL-SDR receiver
  • AI model deployed: Raspberry Pi kit
  • Field day MVP: Dual-band mobile antenna

Purpose: Recognize significant progress, provide useful tools

Tier 3: Apex Awards (Value: $150-400)

Criteria: Exceptional achievement and program completion

  • Capstone project excellence: VHF/UHF mobile transceiver
  • Championship winner: HF QRP transceiver kit
  • General/Extra license: Antenna rotator system
  • Program completion (top 10%): Complete solar power kit
  • Leadership award: Drone (DJI Mini or equivalent)
  • Innovation prize: Advanced SDR (HackRF, LimeSDR)

Purpose: Celebrate mastery, equip for independent operation

Revenue Scenarios

  • 20 students @ $15/mo = $2,700 (9 months)

  • 8 students @ $85/mo = $6,120
  • 2 sponsors @ $150/mo = $2,700

  • Shortfall: $17,694 (requires grants/fundraising)

Fundraising Strategies

Grant Opportunities

  • ARRL Education & Technology Program: Up to $5,000 for youth radio education
  • Local STEM Grants: Schools, municipalities, tech companies ($2,000-10,000)
  • Amateur Radio Clubs: Local club sponsorships ($500-2,000)
  • Corporate Sponsorships: Tech companies, antenna manufacturers ($1,000-5,000)
  • Foundations: STEM education-focused nonprofits (varies widely)

Fundraising Events

Revenue-Generating Activities:

  • Amateur Radio Flea Market: Vendors pay table fees, students assist (target: $2,000)
  • Build-a-thon: Public antenna building workshop, participants pay materials + fee ($1,500)
  • Field Day Public Exhibition: Demonstrations, donations, merchandise ($1,000)
  • Ham Radio License Classes: Charge public for prep courses, students assist teaching ($1,200)
  • Equipment Sales: Build and sell antenna kits, cables, accessories (ongoing, $2,000+)

Equipment Acquisition Strategies

  • Donated Equipment: Contact local hams for surplus gear (50% of radio equipment)
  • Manufacturer Discounts: Educational pricing from Yaesu, Icom, Kenwood (20-30% off)
  • Used Market: QRZ.com, hamfests for budget transceivers (30-50% savings)
  • DIY Kits: QRP transceiver kits vs commercial (60% savings)
  • Bulk Purchasing: Negotiate volume discounts for Raspberry Pi, SDRs

Long-Term Sustainability

Year 2+ Cost Reduction

After initial equipment purchase, annual costs drop significantly:

  • No equipment purchases (already owned)
  • Consumables only: $1,500/year
  • Operations continue: $15,100/year
  • Award pool: $3,000/year
  • Year 2 Total: $19,600 (49% reduction)
  • Cost per student: $392 ($44/month)

Building a Sustainable Club

  1. Alumni Network:
    • Graduates return as assistant instructors
    • Form core of local amateur radio club
    • Provide mentorship to new cohorts
    • Contribute financially when able
  2. Equipment Rotation:
    • Year 1 capstone winners receive apex awards
    • Those devices returned to program after 2 years
    • Cycle continues, reducing replacement costs
  3. Partnerships:
    • School district collaboration (venue, students)
    • University engineering departments (advanced projects)
    • Amateur radio clubs (mentors, equipment loans)
    • Emergency management (training opportunities)
  4. Revenue Diversification:
    • Ongoing fundraising events (not just startup)
    • Equipment rental to other groups
    • Consulting services for schools starting programs
    • Summer intensive programs (additional revenue)
