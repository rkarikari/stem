Aviation Fundamentals for Multirotors

Four Forces of Flight

Quadcopter Flight Dynamics - Four Forces
Four fundamental forces acting on a multirotor drone in forward flight

Understanding Multirotor Dynamics:

  • Lift: Upward force from rotor thrust (must equal weight for hover)
  • Weight: Downward gravitational force (mass × 9.8 m/s²)
  • Thrust: Forward component when drone tilts (controlled by differential rotor speeds)
  • Drag: Air resistance opposing motion (increases with speed)

Flight Control Methods:

  • Hover: All rotors equal speed, total thrust = weight
  • Climb: Increase all rotor speeds equally (thrust > weight)
  • Descend: Decrease all rotor speeds (thrust < weight)
  • Forward: Tilt forward by speeding up rear rotors, slowing front
  • Yaw: Opposite rotor pairs spin at different speeds (torque imbalance)

Drone-Radio Integration Applications

Application 1: Elevated Antenna Platform

Radio Horizon Extension with Drone Altitude
Dramatic range extension achieved by elevating antenna to 100m altitude

Concept: Extending VHF/UHF Range

Theory: Radio horizon = 4.12 × √h kilometers

  • Ground level (h=2m): range ≈ 5.8 km
  • At 100m altitude: range ≈ 41 km (7× improvement!)

Hardware Setup:

  • DJI Phantom 4 or Mavic Pro
  • Handheld VHF transceiver (200g with battery)
  • Lightweight dipole antenna (20g)
  • PTT relay (RC-controlled or autonomous)
  • 3D-printed mounting cradle
  • Power via drone battery or separate LiPo

Operating Modes:

  1. Manual Relay: Pilot triggers PTT via spare channel
  2. Cross-Band Repeat: RX on 2m, TX on 70cm
  3. APRS Beacon: Transmit GPS position every 30 sec

Mission Profile:

  • Climb to 100m AGL
  • Hover or slow orbit for coverage
  • Extend range from 10 km to 50+ km
  • Flight time: 15-18 minutes with payload
  • Coverage area: ~7,850 km² (vs 314 km² ground)

Application 2: Propagation Mapping

RF Coverage Mapping Flight Pattern
Autonomous grid pattern for 3D RF coverage mapping with signal strength measurement at each waypoint

Objective: Create 3D RF Coverage Map

Equipment:

  • Drone with GPS and data logger
  • RF power meter or SDR receiver (RTL-SDR, 50g)
  • VHF/UHF antenna
  • Storage for telemetry data

Procedure:

  1. Ground station transmits continuous carrier
  2. Drone flies programmed grid pattern at multiple altitudes
  3. Records signal strength (RSSI) at each waypoint
  4. GPS tags each measurement
  5. Downloads data, creates heatmap

Analysis:

  • Identify terrain shadowing effects
  • Measure antenna pattern in real environment
  • Determine optimal repeater placement
  • Observe altitude effects on range
  • Validate propagation models

Application 3: Airborne Relay Station

Cross-Band Repeater Configuration
Airborne cross-band repeater extends communication range between ground stations

Temporary Repeater for Field Operations

Concept: Drone carries dual-band radio configured as cross-band repeater

Hardware:

  • Dual-band transceiver (Yaesu FT-8900R or similar)
  • Dual-band antenna system
  • Duplexer or isolated antennas
  • 12V power from drone battery (regulated)
  • VOX or COR for automatic triggering

Configuration:

  • Receive: 146.520 MHz (2m calling freq)
  • Transmit: 446.000 MHz (70cm)
  • Altitude: 100m AGL
  • Coverage: ~50 km radius

Applications:

  • Emergency communications
  • Search and rescue operations
  • Field day extended coverage
  • Special event communications
  • RF propagation demonstrations

Safety Considerations:

  • RF isolation to prevent desensitization
  • Power consumption monitoring (5-15W continuous)
  • Reduced flight time (10-15 min under load)
  • Frequency coordination required
  • Timeout timer (5-minute max transmission)

Mission Planning

Pre-Flight Checklist

⚠️ Never Skip These Steps:

  1. ✅ Check weather (wind <10 m/s, no rain)
  2. ✅ Inspect drone (props, motors, frame)
  3. ✅ Check battery voltage (>3.8V per cell)
  4. ✅ Test RC link and telemetry
  5. ✅ Verify GPS lock (>6 satellites)
  6. ✅ Set home point and RTH altitude
  7. ✅ Brief team on flight plan
  8. ✅ Confirm airspace clearance
  9. ✅ Station visual observer (spotter)
  10. ✅ Test emergency procedures

Battery Management

LiPo Battery Voltage Discharge Curve
LiPo discharge curve showing safe operating zones and critical voltage thresholds

LiPo Battery Care

Critical LiPo Parameters:

  • Voltage per Cell:
    • Fully charged: 4.2V
    • Storage: 3.8V (50% SOC)
    • Nominal: 3.7V
    • Empty: 3.0V (NEVER go below!)
  • C-Rating: Max discharge rate (25C × 5000mAh = 125A max)
  • Balance Charging: Each cell within 0.01V for safety
  • Storage Voltage: 3.8V/cell if unused >3 days
  • Temperature: Never charge below 0°C
  • Charge Rate: 1C standard (5A for 5000mAh)
  • Cycle Life: 300-500 cycles typical

Solar Charging Integration

Field Charging System:

Components:

  • 100W solar panel
  • MPPT controller
  • 35Ah LiFePO4 battery bank
  • Multi-port LiPo charger (4× simultaneous)

Charging Time:

  • 5000mAh 4S LiPo: ~60Wh capacity
  • At 50W solar input (85% efficiency): 1.4 hours per battery
  • Can charge 4 batteries in 6 hours (sunny day)

Field Operations:

  • Deploy solar panel at flight site
  • Continuous battery rotation
  • All-day flight operations possible
  • No grid power required

Safety Protocols

Flight Safety Rules

⚠️ NON-NEGOTIABLE SAFETY RULES:

  • ❌ NEVER fly over people
  • ❌ NEVER fly beyond visual line of sight (VLOS)
  • ❌ NEVER fly near airports without authorization
  • ❌ NEVER fly above 400 ft AGL (unless authorized)
  • ❌ NEVER fly in controlled airspace without clearance
  • ❌ NEVER fly in bad weather (wind, rain, fog)
  • ✅ ALWAYS maintain VLOS (visual line of sight)
  • ✅ ALWAYS have visual observer for complex missions
  • ✅ ALWAYS check NOTAMs before flight
  • ✅ ALWAYS respect privacy (no filming without permission)
  • ✅ ALWAYS plan emergency landing zones
  • ✅ ALWAYS maintain 30% battery reserve for RTH

LiPo Fire Safety

⚠️ LiPo Battery Fire Procedures:

Prevention:

  • Never overcharge (>4.2V per cell)
  • Never overdischarge (<3.0V per cell)
  • Never puncture or crush
  • Store in LiPo-safe bags
  • Charge on non-flammable surface
  • Never leave charging unattended

If Fire Occurs:

  • ❌ DO NOT use water (makes it worse!)
  • ✅ Use sand, dry powder, or CO2 extinguisher
  • ✅ Move away from fire (toxic smoke)
  • ✅ Let it burn out in safe area if cannot extinguish
  • ✅ Ventilate area after fire

Regulations and Licensing

UAS Operating Rules (General Guidelines)

Common Regulations (Vary by Country):

  • Registration: Required for drones >250g
  • Remote Pilot Certificate: Required for commercial operations
  • Altitude Limit: 400 ft (120m) AGL
  • Speed Limit: 100 mph (160 km/h)
  • Visual Line of Sight: Required at all times
  • Airspace Authorization: Required in controlled airspace
  • Night Operations: Requires anti-collision lighting
  • Over People: Generally prohibited (exceptions exist)

Note: Always check local regulations. Requirements vary significantly by country and region.

Hands-On Projects

Project 1: Aerial Photography Mission

Objective:

Document RadioSport field operations from aerial perspective

Mission Plan:

  1. Scout location, identify key features to photograph
  2. Plan flight path (orbits, reveal shots, overhead)
  3. Set camera parameters (4K video, manual exposure)
  4. Execute flight with smooth movements
  5. Download and edit footage
  6. Create promotional video for program

Learning Outcomes:

  • Drone piloting skills
  • Cinematography and composition
  • Video editing
  • Mission planning
  • Documentation techniques

Project 2: Autonomous Inspection Flight

Objective:

Program autonomous mission to inspect antenna array

Requirements:

  • Mission planning software (Litchi or DJI GS Pro)
  • Waypoint programming
  • Camera gimbal control
  • Automated image capture

Mission Steps:

  1. Survey antenna site, identify inspection points
  2. Create waypoint mission (each antenna element)
  3. Program gimbal angles for optimal viewing
  4. Set photo triggers at each waypoint
  5. Execute autonomous mission
  6. Review images for antenna damage or issues

Project 3: Search and Rescue Training

Objective:

Practice locating hidden radio transmitter using drone

Setup:

  • Hide APRS beacon in field (fox hunt)
  • Drone with receiver and direction finding antenna
  • Ground team with handheld receivers

Procedure:

  1. Ground team gets initial bearing to transmitter
  2. Drone launches to altitude for wider search area
  3. Aerial search pattern to narrow location
  4. Ground team approaches based on drone guidance
  5. Locate transmitter, document time and method

Applications:

  • Emergency locator beacon (ELT) searches
  • Lost person with radio
  • Interference source location
  • Coordination of ground and aerial teams
