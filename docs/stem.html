Key Finding — Hardware Costs Strongly Support the Fee Structure

Even after applying a 25% Ghana import surcharge, hardware costs represent just 2.6%–10.4% of monthly fees. The fee model is not merely sustainable — it is well-capitalised. The largest cost driver is teacher stipends (30%), which is intentional and essential to programme quality. Tier 1 SDR devices are provisioned 1:1 per student, matching Tier 2 HT allocation and maximising hands-on learning. The RTL-SDR V4 supply risk (discontinued chip) is mitigated by the readily available V3 at $24.95 and equivalent generic alternatives.

Executive Summary

A new engine for STEM education in Ghana

RadioSport STEM is a nationwide, fee-based Amateur Radio programme integrated across Ghana's education system — from upper primary through JHS, SHS, and beyond. Hardware cost verification confirms 92%+ fee margins on every tier.

Students begin training young and progress through three hardware-driven tiers, gaining practical skills in radio communication, electronics, emergency response, digital technologies, and entrepreneurship. The programme is 100% self-financing through modest monthly student fees — requiring zero donor or government capital after initial setup.

Hardware costs for all three verified devices (RTL-SDR Blog V4, Quansheng UV-K6, Xiegu G106) confirm that equipment represents less than 11% of any monthly fee. The financial model is sound and conservatively structured.

Risk Management

Updated risks — including hardware supply factors

RiskLikelihoodMitigation Strategy
RTL-SDR V4 supply discontinuation Medium R828D chip is out of production; V4 is limited edition. Mitigation: dual-source strategy (RTL-SDR V3 at $24.95 / GH₵275 and generic RTL2832U dongles at $15–20 / GH₵165–220). Functionally equivalent for SDR learning objectives. Programme is not dependent on a single SKU.
Ghana import duties and currency fluctuation Medium 25% import surcharge already built into all hardware cost models in this document. Annual fee review (capped at inflation) adjusts for currency movements. Bulk procurement contracts established in Year 1 can lock in pricing for 12–24 months.
Low parental buy-in or fee resistance Medium Hardware cost transparency (students receive a $50 SDR or $22 HT device for $20–$35/month) is a compelling value argument to parents. Need-based subsidies via PTA; showcase NCA certification value. Entry-tier device cost is verifiably affordable.
Teacher capacity and training gaps Medium Comprehensive national training programme in Year 1. All three verified devices have extensive free online documentation (RTL-SDR.com, Quansheng GitHub, Radioddity/Xiegu). CHIRP software and SDRSharp tutorials are widely available and free.
Equipment theft or damage Medium Low per-unit replacement cost ($22 UV-K6 bulk, $40 RTL-SDR V4) makes theft economically manageable. Regional lab security, student responsibility agreements, and 2% of national office allocation for basic insurance.
Regulatory delays (NCA licensing) Low Early NCA partnership; training aligned to official syllabus. All three devices are internationally certified (CE, FCC, RoHS). Receiver-only Tier 1 (SDR) requires no transmission licence, enabling immediate start.
