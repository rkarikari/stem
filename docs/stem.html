Executive Summary

A new engine for STEM education in Ghana

RadioSport STEM is a nationwide, fee-based Amateur Radio programme delivering $1,000,000 net profit in Year 1 across 50 schools, each running 200 students in 2 clubs. Hardware cost verification confirms 92%+ fee margins on every tier.

Students progress through two hardware-driven tiers. The programme generates $4,800,000 gross revenue in Year 1. After teacher stipends (30%), school equipment (15%), national office (10%), and regional labs (15%), a 30% net profit margin yields $1,440,000 — in Year 1 alone.

Hardware costs for both verified devices (Quansheng UV-K6 at 2.6% of fee, Xiegu G106 at 4.9% of fee) confirm outstanding margins at every tier. The financial model is sound, conservative, and profit-positive from Year 1.

Risk Management

Updated risks — including hardware supply factors

RiskLikelihoodMitigation Strategy
UV-K6 / HT supply disruption Medium Quansheng UV-K6 is widely available via Alibaba in large quantities at $12–15/unit. Multiple suppliers confirmed. Generic UV-K5/K6 equivalents available as backup. Programme is not dependent on a single SKU — any CHIRP-compatible VHF/UHF HT is a functional substitute.
Ghana import duties and currency fluctuation Medium 25% import surcharge already built into all hardware cost models in this document. Annual fee review (capped at inflation) adjusts for currency movements. Bulk procurement contracts established in Year 1 can lock in pricing for 12–24 months.
Low parental buy-in or fee resistance Medium Hardware cost transparency (students receive a $22 HT or shared HF station for $35–$55/month) is a compelling value argument to parents. Need-based subsidies via PTA; showcase NCA certification value. Both tiers have hardware costs under 5% of the monthly fee.
Teacher capacity and training gaps Medium Comprehensive national training programme in Year 1. Both verified devices have extensive free online documentation (Quansheng GitHub, Radioddity/Xiegu). CHIRP software tutorials are widely available and free.
Equipment theft or damage Medium Low per-unit replacement cost ($22 UV-K6 bulk) makes HT theft economically manageable. HF stations are shared and secured on school premises. Regional lab security, student responsibility agreements, and 2% of national office allocation for basic insurance.
Regulatory delays (NCA licensing) Low Early NCA partnership; training aligned to official syllabus. Both devices are internationally certified (CE, FCC, RoHS). NCA licensing is integrated into the curriculum from the first term.
